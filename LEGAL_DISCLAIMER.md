## üö® REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS

### Prediction Markets

**United States:**
- Prediction markets are regulated by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)
- Event contracts may be deemed illegal gambling under state laws
- Only CFTC-registered Designated Contract Markets (DCMs) or CFTC-approved entities may legally operate certain prediction markets
- Violating these regulations can result in federal criminal charges

**Required Registrations:**
- CFTC Registration (if offering commodity derivatives)
- Money Service Business (MSB) registration with FinCEN
- State money transmitter licenses (varies by state)
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance program
- Know Your Customer (KYC) verification system

### Sports Betting & Sportsbook Integration

**United States:**
- Sports betting is regulated at the state level following the repeal of PASPA
- Only licensed operators in jurisdictions that have legalized sports betting may accept wagers
- Unlicensed sports betting is a federal crime under the Wire Act and UIGEA
- Each state has different licensing requirements and tax structures

**Critical Issues with This Platform:**
1. **Credential Storage**: Storing user credentials for third-party sportsbook accounts may violate those platforms' Terms of Service
2. **Automated Access**: Programmatic access to sportsbook accounts may be prohibited
3. **Unauthorized Access**: Accessing accounts without explicit API agreements may violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
4. **Aiding Violations**: Facilitating circumvention of platform controls may expose you to liability

**State Licensing:**
- You need separate licenses in EACH state where you operate
- Licensing costs range from $10,000 to $500,000+ per state
- Requires background checks, financial audits, and compliance infrastructure
